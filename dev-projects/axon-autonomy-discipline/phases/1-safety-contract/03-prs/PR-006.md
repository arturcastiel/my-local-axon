# PR-006 — Cadence backstop (the teeth)

- **Status:** spec
- **Phase:** 1-safety-contract
- **Complexity:** M
- **Depends on:** PR-005 (the cadence state), PR-002 (the reanchor that records its fire)
- **Why:** Turn the cadence STATE into ENFORCEMENT. The reanchor cadence cannot be "trusted to fire" (the
  project's whole thesis); the backstop makes a lapse DETECTABLE at the gate — detection = enforced. This
  is the VERIFY half of the mechanism/signal split (PR-005 = the state; PR-006 = the teeth).

## Acceptance criteria
1. `autonomy_cadence.check(ws, every=5)` → `{lapsed: bool, since: int, reason}`: lapsed when
   `since_reanchor ≥ every` (the reanchor is overdue). Pure read.
2. `r_autonomy_cadence.check(ctx)` → Violation when **an autonomous run** (grant active) reaches the gate
   with the cadence **lapsed** (`since ≥ every`). Silent when not autonomous, or not lapsed, or no
   changeset. WARN by default; BLOCK via `L:autonomy-cadence-required`.
3. After a reanchor fires (records → `since=0`), the rule is silent — proving the loop: fire resets,
   lapse re-arms.
